About Gina

From the time Gina Chee was a little kid, she had a passion for drawing. From early elementary school, she began learning graphite, pastel, and watercolor and later studied painting in middle school, with studio art teacher Xinsheng Wang. It was there where she fell in love with acrylic and oil painting, and it quickly became her most favorite and strongest mediums. In high school, Gina's technical skills and aesthetic further developed, as she joined her high school's strong art program. Her art creativity and personal style is heavily influenced by many different facet of her life experience. These include 8 years of piano and music training and practice, 8 years of dancing, cultural appreciation from many travel experience to different countries. She has won numerous awards for her art work, including Gold and Silver Keys in the Regional Scholastic Art and Writing Awards, 1st place Houston Young Artist competition, Finalist in the Houston Livestock Show and Rodeo School Art Contest, State qualifier in Visual Arts Scholastic Contest etc. She earned grade 5 on her AP Studio Art Portfolio.

Since Gina took her first computer science class, it opened a new digital world to her. The more she learned, the more interest she got in. It is not only the joy from building a program using Java language or the web development tools, but also the logical thinking and problem solving skill she gained to help her long run. She plans to pursue a major in college combining Computer Science and Art.

Gina loves to use her art and music skill to help to make the community better. She is an active member of National Art Honor society. Volunteered many hours to local events such as face-painting at elementary schools, assisting at the Bayou City Art Festival, designing and painting her school’s homecoming and International Festival murals, school’s annual Retrospective art show’s banners. She volunteered her summer to teach kindergarten kids art class in China. Organized Music Doing Good events to promote music to community. It's because of the love and encouragement through her family, friends, peers, and school teachers that Gina has been able to continuously do what she loves most.
